What is E-Waste?

E-waste is any discarded electronic equipment. These items contain both valuable materials worth recovering and hazardous materials that can harm our environment and health if not disposed of correctly.

Why Recycle?

Recycling recovers precious metals, saves energy, reduces greenhouse gas emissions, and protects our planet.

How & Where to Recycle

A Simple Checklist:

  • Back up your personal data.
  • Wipe your device by performing a factory reset.
  • Remove SIM cards and memory cards.
  • DO NOT throw e-waste in regular dustbins.
  • DO NOT sell to informal scrap dealers.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is this service free?

Yes, our awareness project and collection drives are a community service and completely free.

What happens to the e-waste you collect?

All collected items are handed over to government-authorized e-waste recycling partners who process them safely.

How should I handle my personal data?

Always back up any data you want to save and then perform a 'Factory Reset' on your device to wipe all personal information before recycling it.
